About Me

Engineer. Athlete. Builder.

Engine System — Connecting Rod Force Analysis

GoalAnalyze forces on a connecting rod in a crank-piston engine. Crank AB: constant 2000 rpm. Connecting rod BD: 4 lb. Piston P: 5 lb.
What I DidModeled crank, connecting rod, and piston in OnShape with full joint constraints and animated the assembly. Applied Newton's 2nd Law for rigid bodies in MATLAB to solve for pin forces at B and D across crank positions.
Physical Build3D-printed all parts from OnShape STLs using Bambu Studio. Built a Lego version to validate kinematic behavior. Both physical models matched the simulation motion.
Successfully determined dynamic forces at B and D. Physical build confirmed — MATLAB outputs validated at multiple crank angles.

03
Mech. DesignFEAOnShape

Door Handle Failure Analysis

GoalDesign a door handle in OnShape, apply bearing loads, run Von Mises stress FEA, then redesign to reduce failure risk.
What I DidApplied fixed constraints and bearing forces. Von Mises criterion predicts yielding in ductile metals under tension/compression. Adjusted handle length and thickness to reduce stress concentrations. Next: test alternative base-plate materials and rerun simulation.
Iterative redesign reduced peak stress concentrations — demonstrates full engineering design loop from model to failure to fix.

SolarIQ — Intelligent Solar Insights

GoalReal-time sensor system measuring temperature and light intensity to study factors affecting solar panel efficiency.
How It WorksPhotoresistor + thermistor voltage dividers on breadboard → NI myDAQ captures analog voltages → LabVIEW block diagram applies the Beta Parameter equation to compute live temperature in °C and °F. Co-developed with Hunter Boucher.
Live dashboard displaying verified temp and light readings — validated against reference measurements.
